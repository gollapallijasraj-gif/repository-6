How to deal with scammers

If you are getting mysterious calls from random people asking for banking details or asking for money and following a series of steps, here's what you should do to deter them:

1. The Fact-Check (MouthShut.com)

Step away from the live conversation and check an independent platform. platforms like MouthShut and similar consumer complaint forums are filled with real-time reports from other targets.

2. The Reverse Interrogation

Scammers operate using strict scripts designed to keep you on the defensive or feeling hurried. When you started asking for their employee IDs, official email addresses, or specific details about your actual policy that only a real LIC system would know. because time is money for them, they cut the call to move on to someone less cautious.

A Quick Reality Check on Insurance:

Real insurance companies or regulatory bodies like IRDAI will never call you offering bonuses, commission returns, or refunds that require you to pay money upfront via a random link. Any legitimate refund is processed directly into your registered bank account via NEFT.
